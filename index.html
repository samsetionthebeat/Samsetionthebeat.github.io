ICT CONCEPTS
EXPLAINED

Every key concept taught by Michael J. Huddleston — simplified, structured, and applied to real markets.

🏦
Order Blocks

Identify the last down/up candle before a strong institutional move. These zones act as magnets for price to revisit and react.

Foundational
💧
Liquidity

Understand where stop losses cluster — above highs and below lows — and how institutions hunt these pools before reversing.

Advanced
⚖️
Fair Value Gaps

Three-candle imbalances where price moves so fast it leaves inefficiency. Price seeks to fill these gaps during retracements.

Intermediate
🔮
Breaker Blocks

A failed order block that transforms into a breaker. When price sweeps a high/low and the OB fails, a breaker is born.

Advanced
🎯
Market Structure

Learn to read Higher Highs, Higher Lows, and structural shifts (CHoCH & BOS) to stay aligned with the trend.

Foundational
🕐
Killzones & Sessions

London, New York, and Asian killzones — timing your entries around high-probability windows of institutional activity.

Strategy
📐
Optimal Trade Entry

The OTE retracement zone (61.8%–79%) based on Fibonacci, used by ICT to pinpoint high-precision entry levels.

Intermediate
🌊
Mitigation Blocks

After a failed order block, price returns to mitigate the imbalance. Learn to identify and trade these re-entries.

Advanced
🔄
Power of 3 (AMD)

Accumulation → Manipulation → Distribution. The 3-phase daily model that institutions use to move markets.
